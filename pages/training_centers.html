Hidaka Training and Research Center

The Hidaka Training and Research Center is a specialized facility dedicated to advancing knowledge, skill development, and innovation. Situated in a region with a strong academic and technical heritage, the center functions as a hub for practical training and scientific research, serving professionals, students, and researchers alike. The center is equipped with modern laboratories, simulation environments, and collaborative workspaces that enable hands-on learning and real-world application of theoretical concepts. Its programs are designed to combine classroom instruction with experiential practice so participants can immediately apply new skills in professional settings. Research at the Hidaka Center spans multiple disciplines and frequently involves partnerships with universities, industry experts, and government institutions. This interdisciplinary approach promotes innovation, supports sustainable development, and helps raise industry standards through evidence-based findings and technology transfer. In addition to technical training and research, the center emphasizes professional growth and international collaboration. It regularly hosts workshops, conferences, and exchange programs that foster knowledge sharing and build networks across sectors and countries. Miyazaki Yearling Training Farm

The Miyazaki Yearling Training Farm is a modern equine development facility focused on the early training and conditioning of young thoroughbreds. Located in the warm, mild climate of Miyazaki, Japan, the farm provides an ideal environment for raising and preparing yearlings for future racing and performance careers. Equipped with advanced training tracks, spacious paddocks, and veterinary support facilities, the farm ensures that each horse receives balanced physical development and professional care. Trainers and staff at Miyazaki combine traditional horsemanship with modern training methods to nurture the athletic potential and temperament of every young horse. The farm's research and data-driven approach to equine management help refine training programs, monitor progress, and improve overall performance outcomes. Nutrition, health monitoring, and individualized training schedules are all key aspects of daily operations. In addition to its focus on horse training, the Miyazaki Yearling Training Farm serves as a center for education and professional development in the equine industry. It regularly hosts seminars, demonstrations, and collaborative projects with breeders, veterinarians, and academic institutions. Through its dedication to innovation, welfare, and excellence, the Miyazaki Yearling Training Farm plays an important role in shaping the next generation of thoroughbreds and advancing equine training standards in Japan and beyond.
